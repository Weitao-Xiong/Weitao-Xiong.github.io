Abstract

High-fidelity simulation of mmWave radar signals for dynamic human motion is valuable for developing radar-based human sensing models, but collecting accurately labeled measurements for each deployment site remains expensive.

HybridSim synthesizes mmWave radar signals from dynamic human meshes under a fixed indoor room configuration by decoupling propagation into direct inverse-rendering paths and indirect 3DGS-based paths. This hybrid design improves agreement with physically based references while keeping site-specific data augmentation practical.

Method Overview

Direct Radar Path

The primary reflection path is modeled with inverse rendering and a microfacet BRDF over the moving human surface.

Indirect Multipath

3D Gaussian Splatting and virtual receiver geometry approximate room-specific multipath interference efficiently.
